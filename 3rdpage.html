Facts About Evolutionary Ecology

Evolutionary ecology is the study of how ecological processes such as competition, predation, and mutualism influence the evolution of species. Enter a number(1-10) to know interesting facts!

  • Mutualism, where two species benefit from each other, can drive evolutionary changes. For example, flowering plants and their pollinators often evolve together.
  • Predation pressure can lead to the evolution of defensive traits in prey species. Some butterflies, for example, have evolved to mimic toxic species to avoid being eaten.
